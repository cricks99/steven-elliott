Business Success

The American Dream in Action

From Military to Civilian Career

After completing his military service, Steven went to work in the private sector as a personal trainer and selling cars at a local car dealership in Royal Oak.

These experiences gave Steven valuable insights into customer service, sales, and the challenges facing everyday Americans trying to make an honest living.

Taking the Entrepreneurial Leap

In 2001, Steven took a bold step that would define his business career. He took out a loan on his home to start his own business, American Pride Tattoo Studio.

Innovation and Growth

Steven made the innovative decision to not only offer tattoos, but also tattoo removal in his shops. This forward-thinking approach demonstrated his ability to identify market opportunities and adapt to customer needs.

Quickly seeing the success of offering aesthetics work, in 2012, Steven founded a full service aesthetics center, which he owns and operates to this day: Rochester Laser Center.

Investing in People

Steven has enjoyed a successful business career by investing in any businesses' most valuable asset: people. He offered individuals the opportunity to learn a trade and build a career.

Through his businesses, Steven has given back to his community by contributing to many local events, charities, and organizations. This commitment to community service reflects his belief that successful businesses have a responsibility to give back.

Business Lessons for Congress

Steven's business experience has taught him valuable lessons that he brings to his campaign for Congress:

Fiscal Responsibility: Running a successful business requires careful financial management and living within your means.
Regulatory Burden: Excessive government regulations can stifle small businesses and prevent job creation.
Job Creation: Small businesses are the backbone of America's economy and the primary source of new jobs.
Customer Service: Success comes from serving others and meeting their needs effectively.
