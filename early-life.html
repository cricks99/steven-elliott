Humble Beginnings

Steven Elliott was born in Windsor, Ontario. His father was a Canadian construction worker, and his mother was an American secretary.

His family lived in the Detroit area until he was 3 years old. They then moved to Colorado for his father's work as a union Glazier for 4 years. At the age of 8, Steven and his family returned to the Detroit area, purchasing a home in Roseville.

Learning the Value of Hard Work

Steven learned that if he wanted anything in life, he had to work for it. He started working at the age of 12 and never stopped.

Foundation of Success

These early experiences taught Steven invaluable lessons about responsibility, dedication, and the dignity of honest work. The work ethic developed during these formative years would become the foundation for everything he accomplished later in life - his military service, business success, and commitment to public service.

Steven's childhood demonstrates the American values of self-reliance and determination that he brings to his campaign for Congress.
